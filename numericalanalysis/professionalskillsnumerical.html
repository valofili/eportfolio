Professional Skills Matrix - Numeric Analysis

This appendix presents a structured overview of the professional and technical skills developed during the Numerical Analysis module, alongside evidence of learning and planned actions for continued development.

The matrix demonstrates how learning outcomes from the module have translated into practical competencies and supports reflective evaluation of professional growth

Skill Area Current Proficiency Level Evidence from the Module Application and Action Plan
Statistical Reasoning Strong Demonstrated correct selection and application of descriptive and inferential statistical methods, including t-tests, chi-square tests, ANOVA, and regression analysis. Confident interpretation of p-values and confidence intervals in coursework. Apply statistical reasoning consistently in future AI and data science modules, particularly when evaluating model performance and experimental results.
R Programming for Data Analysis Confident Successfully used R to import datasets, clean data, generate summary statistics, create contingency tables, and perform regression modelling. Produced reproducible analyses aligned with best practice. Expand R proficiency by exploring advanced packages such as tidyverse, broom, and ggplot2 to enhance data wrangling and reporting efficiency.
Data Visualisation Strong Created histograms, boxplots, bar charts, stacked bar charts, and correlation heatmaps to support exploratory and inferential analysis. Used visuals to justify statistical decisions. Focus on producing publication-quality visualisations and learning principles of visual clarity for professional and academic reporting.
Interpretation and Communication of Results Developing Improved ability to explain statistical findings in written and oral form, moving beyond reporting significance to discussing effect size, uncertainty, and practical implications. Continue developing narrative-driven reporting skills by explicitly linking statistical outputs to research questions and real-world implications.
Critical Evaluation of Statistical Outputs Developing Engaged with limitations of statistical methods, including assumptions, outliers, and model fit, informed by tutor feedback and reflective practice. Strengthen critical evaluation skills by routinely checking assumptions and incorporating sensitivity analyses where appropriate.
Independent and Reflective Learning Strong Demonstrated independent engagement with core textbooks and supplementary resources, including R-ticulate and biostatistics literature. Maintained reflective awareness of learning progress and challenges. Maintain a reflective learning journal across future modules to document challenges, feedback, and strategies for continuous improvement.
